## Core idea

Rejects linear AI maturity models. Instead maps organizations on a 2D archetype space defined by two axes — Coordination (Structured → Emergent) and Control (Centralized → Decentralized) — and diagnoses agentic readiness from where an organization actually sits on that map. AI transformation is not a journey from left to right; it starts from your current organizational DNA.

### Connection to our work

Powerful complement to our framework. Where our template uses Wardley Mapping to place capabilities on a genesis-to-commodity axis, Appelo adds a second diagnostic dimension: organizational structure DNA. The five archetypes predict how much agentic AI an organization can absorb — not as a ceiling to accept but as a constraint to design around.

Key tensions with our approach:
- Appelo's framework is diagnostic/descriptive; ours is transformational — they compose well
- His "Zone of Contradiction" (Structured + Decentralized) maps onto the most common transformation failure pattern we see: teams get autonomy but processes stay rigid
- "Cell Structure" organizations (Holacracy, Buurtzorg, Morning Star) are the natural landing zone for AI-first design — aligns with [Reinventing organizations: geillustreerde versie (Dutch Edition)](../books/laloux-reinventing-organizations-geillustreerde-versie-dutch-editio.md) and [Organize for Complexity: How to Get Life Back Into Work to Build the High-Performance Organization (Betacodex Publishing)](../books/pflaeging-organize-for-complexity-how-to-get-life-back-into-work-to-bu.md)

The **Human-Robot-Agent** framing (from his companion book) is the strongest vocabulary yet for describing the new workforce composition in Section 8 (Greenfield) and Section 9 (AI-First Design Decisions) of the [AI-First Value Stream Assessment](../../AI-First Value Stream Assessment Template.md).
