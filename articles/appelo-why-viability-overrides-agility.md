## Core argument

Organizations obsessed with agility (speed, responsiveness) miss a more important threat: long-term survival. Pure agility produces short-term wins that destroy future capability.

Two concrete AI examples:
1. **AI replacing junior workers** — immediate efficiency gain, future talent pipeline destroyed
2. **AI code generation** — rapid prototyping produces unmaintainable code nobody can fix later

**Viability** = the ability to survive and sustain itself over the long term, preserving identity while remaining financially and operationally sound. Unlike agility's focus on speed, viability emphasizes **calibration over acceleration**.

Agility should be a *component* of viability, not the primary goal. Sometimes waiting "until the dust has settled" is the right strategic move.

## Key metaphor

The cockroach business wins by being resilient, not merely fast.
